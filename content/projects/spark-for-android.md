As Android Engineering Lead at [Readdle](https://readdle.com/), I led the team that brought [Spark Mail](https://sparkmailapp.com/) to Android - sharing a single Swift codebase for business logic across iOS, Android, Mac, and Windows. We built the [Swift Android Toolchain](https://github.com/readdle/swift-android-toolchain), an open-source stack of seven repositories covering everything from the SDK and JNI code generation to [NDK bindings](https://github.com/andriydruk/swift-android-ndk) and [build tools](https://github.com/readdle/swift-android-buildtools).

Over seven years the platform matured from a forked compiler to upstream Swift 6 with all architecture targets. Together with the Swift Android community, this work contributed to Swift on Android becoming an [officially supported platform](https://www.swift.org/documentation/articles/swift-sdk-for-android-getting-started.html) on swift.org and the formation of the [Swift Android Working Group](https://www.swift.org/android-workgroup/).
